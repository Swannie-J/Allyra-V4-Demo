Extend the Allyra prototype to simulate the next stages of the experience after the tour.

Continue from the current screen where Allyra has said:

“Alright — that's the quick tour.
Now let's start with forming an understanding of your business.”

Design a realistic chat conversation flow between Allyra and the user that gathers basic business information.

The conversation should feel natural and friendly, not like a questionnaire.

Example conversation flow:

Allyra asks:
“First, tell me a little about your business. What do you do and who are your main customers?”

User responds.

Allyra follows up with a few natural questions such as:
• What sector your business operates in
• How the business currently makes money
• Rough size or stage of the business
• What you feel is currently limiting growth

Do not create long forms. These should appear as normal chat messages in the conversation.

After several messages, Allyra responds with something like:

“Great — that gives me a good starting picture of your business.

To go deeper and generate meaningful insights, I’ll also review some documents and run market research.”

Then Allyra requests documents:

“Could you please upload any of the following if you have them available?

• recent bank statements
• financial statements or management accounts
• major sales contracts or offtake agreements
• any strategy or business planning documents”

A document upload component should appear in the chat input area.

When the user uploads files and sends them, simulate the journey progression:

Update the journey tracker so the active stage moves from:

Understand Your Business → Analyse Your Business.

After documents are uploaded, simulate analysis activity inside the chat interface.

Add small system-style status messages such as:

• Running financial analysis
• Reviewing uploaded documents
• Researching your market
• Analysing sector trends
• Reviewing competitor signals

These should appear as subtle “analysis in progress” messages or small loading indicators inside the chat.

This stage should make the product feel intelligent and active.

After the simulated analysis completes, the system should prepare to transition into the next stage of the journey: Growth Insights.
