Continue the Allyra prototype by designing the **Business Insights** and **Market Intelligence** sections in the left navigation menu.

These sections should now be fully populated based on the conversation and analysis that has already taken place in the Control Room.

The design should feel **clean, premium, and easy for SME founders to understand**. Avoid clutter and avoid long walls of text.

---

BUSINESS INSIGHTS SECTION

When the user clicks **Business Insights**, show a structured dashboard of the insights Allyra has generated.

Display a set of large insight cards, each representing one of the key insight areas discussed in the conversation.

The cards should include:

WHAT'S WORKING
WHAT NEEDS ATTENTION
GROWTH OPPORTUNITIES
THINGS TO WATCH
FINANCIAL INSIGHTS
CURRENT GROWTH BOTTLENECKS

Each card should show:

• A short summary of the key insight points (3–4 bullet points)
• A small icon indicating the type of insight
• A **“Read more” button**

When the user clicks **Read more**, expand the card or open a detailed panel showing deeper analysis.

The expanded view should include:

• More detailed explanation of the insight
• Supporting reasoning from the analysis
• Example implications for the business
• Possible actions or considerations

Example:

FINANCIAL INSIGHTS (card preview)

Revenue concentration among top buyers
Seasonal working capital pressure
Margin improvement potential through premium products

Read more →

Expanded financial analysis could show:

Revenue concentration indicator
Working capital cycle explanation
Margin analysis and potential improvement opportunities

The Business Insights page should feel like the **analytical brain of Allyra**.

---

MARKET INTELLIGENCE SECTION

When the user clicks **Market Intelligence**, show a curated intelligence dashboard about the SME’s market and sector.

The purpose of this section is to show useful external insights the SME may not easily access themselves.

Create several cards displaying different types of intelligence:

SECTOR TRENDS

Growth in demand for natural and healthy snack products
Consumer preference shifting toward clean ingredient products
Retailers expanding shelf space for healthier snack options

COMPETITOR SIGNALS

Emerging regional snack brands entering the natural fruit snack category
Large food companies launching healthier snack lines
Increased competition in supermarket snack aisles

CUSTOMER TRENDS

Rising demand from tourism and hospitality channels
Growing interest in locally produced and natural food products
Premium snack packaging gaining popularity

DISTRIBUTION OPPORTUNITIES

Regional retail chains expanding local supplier programs
Farm stall and tourism retail channels growing
Potential online and direct-to-consumer opportunities

EXPORT SIGNALS

Increasing international demand for natural dried fruit products
Potential niche export markets in Europe and the Middle East

Each intelligence card should contain:

• A short insight summary
• Supporting context
• Optional “Explore further” link or button

The Market Intelligence page should feel like **a live market briefing for the SME’s sector**.

---

DESIGN NOTES

Keep the interface minimal and structured.

Use clear cards, subtle icons, and clean typography.

Avoid dashboards that look overly technical.

The goal is for an SME founder to quickly understand:

• what is happening in their business
• what is happening in their market
• where opportunities may exist

These sections should feel like **valuable strategic intelligence rather than static reports**.
