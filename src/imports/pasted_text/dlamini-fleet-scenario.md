SECTION 4 · Scripted Conversation
TURN 1 — What the business does

Allyra: Tell me a bit about the business. What do you do, and who are your customers?

Owner: We do transport and deliveries. Mostly regional loads around KZN, sometimes Gauteng as well. Our clients are mainly wholesalers, distributors, a couple of manufacturers, and some retail supply jobs.

TURN 2 — History and origin

Allyra: And how long have you been running it, and how did it start?

Owner: I started with one truck about six years ago. At first it was ad hoc loads and subcontracting. Then over time we picked up more regular clients and added more vehicles.

TURN 3 — Revenue sources

Allyra: Which part of the business brings in most of the money now?

Owner: The contract work. That’s where most of the revenue comes from. The once-off loads help fill gaps, but the regular client runs are really what keep the business going.

TURN 4 — Scale and founder dependence

Allyra: How big is the business now, and how much still depends on you personally?

Owner: We’ve got six trucks running. Eleven people altogether. I’m still very involved though. I deal with clients, vehicle issues, driver problems, payments, and anything that goes wrong on the road.

TURN 5 — Growth ambition

Allyra: Over the next year, what are you most trying to improve?

Owner: I want more stability. Better cash flow, fewer breakdown disruptions, and to be able to take on bigger work without stressing every month about fuel and salaries.

TURN 6 — Pressure points

Allyra: When things feel tight, what usually causes it most?

Owner: Cash flow. Always cash flow. The work is there, but clients can take 30, 45, even 60 days to pay. Meanwhile fuel, tyres, repairs, and wages don’t wait.

TURN 7 — Record-keeping

Allyra: Do you keep proper records, or is it more informal, like bank statements, receipts, and what you track yourself?

Owner: The accounts are proper enough. We’ve got bank statements, invoices, client statements, and the accountant does the books. We also track mileage, fuel, and deliveries, but it’s not all in one clean system.

TURN 8 — Binding constraint

Allyra: When the business gets busier, what breaks first?

Owner: Working capital and fleet reliability. More work sounds good, but if two vehicles need attention and clients are paying late, you feel it straight away.
