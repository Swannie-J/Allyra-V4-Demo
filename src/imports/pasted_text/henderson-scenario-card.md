SECTION 4 · Scripted Conversation
TURN 1 — What the business does

Allyra: Tell me a bit about the business. What do you do, and who are your customers?

Owner: We do general steel fabrication. Gates, balustrades, staircases, light structural work, brackets, that sort of thing. Most of our work comes from building contractors, some factory clients, and then the odd private client.

TURN 2 — History and origin

Allyra: And how long have you been running it, and how did it start?

Owner: I started it about eight years ago. I’d worked in fabrication for years before that, then went on my own. It was mostly smaller residential work at first, and over time we got more contractor and industrial jobs.

TURN 3 — Revenue sources

Allyra: Which part of the business brings in most of the money now?

Owner: The contractor work, definitely. That’s where most of the turnover sits. The smaller private jobs help with cash flow, but the contractor jobs are the bigger numbers, even if they’re not always the easiest.

TURN 4 — Scale and founder dependence

Allyra: And how big is the business these days? How many people are involved, and how much still depends on you directly?

Owner: We’ve got fourteen people in total. I’ve got a foreman on the floor and admin in the office, but I still handle most of the quoting, pricing, supplier issues, and anything that gets sticky with clients or jobs.

TURN 5 — Growth ambition

Allyra: Over the next year, what are you most trying to improve?

Owner: Better margins, better control in the workshop, and less firefighting. We’re busy enough. I’m not desperate for more work. I want the business to run better and make the right money.

TURN 6 — Pressure points

Allyra: When things feel tight, what usually causes it most?

Owner: Bad quoting, jobs taking longer than they should, and then late payment from contractors. One or two jobs go wrong and suddenly everything feels tight.

TURN 7 — Record-keeping

Allyra: Do you keep proper records, or is it more informal, like bank statements and what you track yourself?

Owner: The accounts are all proper. Accountant handles the books, VAT, year-end, all of that. But proper job costing is weak. We quote, we invoice, but we don’t always track properly afterwards what each job actually made us.

TURN 8 — Binding constraint

Allyra: When the business gets busier, what breaks first?

Owner: Planning, without a doubt. The workshop gets congested, jobs overlap, things get delayed, and then I get dragged back into sorting everything out.
