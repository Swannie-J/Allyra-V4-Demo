Main dashboard content:

Section 1: Executive summary row
Create 6 elegant summary cards showing:

Total SMEs in portfolio: 200

Finance-ready SMEs

SMEs requiring technical assistance

SMEs in active funding pipeline

SMEs with elevated risk signals

Jobs supported

These cards should be compact, sophisticated, and easy to scan.
They should look clickable, with the suggestion that each opens a deeper filtered portfolio view.

Section 2: Portfolio composition
Create a two-column section.

Left:

Portfolio by sector chart

Example sectors: agri-processing, manufacturing, retail, services, logistics, energy

Right:

Portfolio by geography chart

Regional distribution for an African SME portfolio

Optional chips for country breakdown

Also include a small row of secondary structure indicators beneath or beside these charts:

Export vs domestic mix

Business age mix

Urban vs rural footprint

Supply-chain participation

This section should feel high-level and strategic, not overloaded.

Section 3: Readiness and progression
Create a wide section showing how SMEs are distributed across stages such as:

Early stage

Growth stage

Expansion stage

Finance-ready

Post-investment support

Also show a progression funnel or pathway such as:

Diagnosed

In support

Investment-ready

In funding process

Funded

This section should communicate movement across the portfolio, not just static counts.
It should feel like Allyra is helping the institution move SMEs from diagnosis to capital readiness.

Section 4: Capability overview
Create a clean institutional section showing 5 compact capability pillars across the portfolio:

Strategy

Finance

Sales & Market Access

Operations

HR / Workforce

This can be shown as 5 scorecards, 5 horizontal bars, or another refined summary format.
Do not make this too visually heavy.
It should suggest that the institution can click into each capability pillar to see underlying SMEs and common gaps.

Section 5: Risk and support priorities
Create a balanced two-column section.

Left:
Top portfolio bottlenecks across SMEs, for example:

working capital pressure

weak records and reporting

market access constraints

founder dependence

operational capacity

customer concentration

Right:
Early warning signals panel, for example:

cash flow stress

declining sales momentum

execution delays

documentation gaps

margin pressure

Use clean ranked lists, bars, or compact institutional visuals.
Avoid overly complex graphs.

Section 6: Technical assistance and intervention tracking
Create a practical section showing:

SMEs currently in technical assistance

TA by type: financial management, market access, operations, governance, investment readiness

Intervention status: planned, in progress, completed

A small panel called “Where Allyra recommends intervention now”

This section should feel operational and useful for a portfolio manager trying to decide where support resources should go.

Section 7: Impact and inclusion
Create a refined section showing 5 compact metrics such as:

Women-led SMEs

Youth-led SMEs

SMEs in underserved regions

Average revenue growth across portfolio

Average employment growth

This section should feel integrated into the institutional portfolio logic, not like a separate ESG marketing layer.
It should communicate development and inclusion outcomes in a serious, measured way.

Section 8: Priority SMEs table
At the bottom, create a clean working table of 8 to 10 sample SMEs with columns such as:

SME name

Country

Sector

Stage

Allyra readiness score

Main bottleneck

Current support track

Funding pathway

Risk status

Use subtle colour coding for readiness and risk.
This table is one of the most important parts of the dashboard.
It should feel like a live portfolio management tool, not just a visual report.

Right-side contextual panel or slim insight drawer:
Add a slim right-side panel called “Allyra Portfolio Signals” with 4 short AI-generated institutional observations such as:

“32 SMEs are commercially active but not yet finance-ready due to reporting gaps.”

“Agri-processing shows the strongest near-term funding potential.”

“Technical assistance demand is concentrated in working capital management and market access.”

“12 SMEs have elevated execution risk and may need proactive intervention.”

This panel should feel intelligent and useful, but not flashy or gimmicky.

Interaction model:

Clicking a summary card opens a filtered portfolio breakdown

Clicking a sector or geography chart opens a portfolio composition detail view

Clicking the readiness and progression section opens a deeper diagnostic and stage analysis page

Clicking a capability pillar opens a view of SMEs with that capability gap

Clicking TA sections opens intervention tracking and support allocation detail

Clicking the impact section opens demographic and development outcome detail

Clicking an SME row opens an SME profile page with Allyra insights, bottlenecks, support history, readiness notes, and funding pathway status
